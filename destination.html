Destinations in Malaysia

Explore the diverse and captivating destinations Malaysia has to offer

Kuala Lumpur

The bustling capital city with iconic landmarks like the Petronas Towers and vibrant street markets.

Visit the Petronas Towers

The Petronas Towers are a major landmark of Kuala Lumpur, along with the nearby Kuala Lumpur Tower and Merdeka 118, and are visible in many places across the city.

Explore Batu Caves

A limestone outcrop located just north of Kuala Lumpur, Batu Caves has three main caves featuring temples and Hindu shrines. Its main attraction is the large statue of the Hindu God at the entrance, besides a steep 272 climb up its steps to finally view the stunning skyline of the city centre.

Shop at Central Market

Built in 1888 as a wet market, Central Market with significant historical value, today is the centre for Malaysian culture, art, and craft. A must-see destination for international travellers and domestic tourists.

Accommodations

Luxury Hotels

Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia's bustling capital, is renowned for its luxury accommodations that offer world-class amenities and unparalleled comfort. Whether you're looking for modern elegance or timeless sophistication, Kuala Lumpur's luxury hotels provide a lavish retreat in the heart of the city.

Budget Hostels

Kuala Lumpur offers a vibrant array of budget hostels that provide comfortable and affordable accommodations for travelers. Whether you're a backpacker, student, or simply looking to save on lodging, these hostels offer a friendly atmosphere, convenient locations, and essential amenities.

Penang

An island known for its rich cultural heritage, street art, and mouthwatering cuisine.

Wander through George Town's UNESCO sites

Immerse yourself in the charm of George Town's UNESCO-listed heritage zone, featuring colonial-era architecture, Clan Jetties, temples, and more.

Enjoy local street food

From savory dishes like char kway teow and nasi kandar to sweet treats like cendol and apom manis, Penang's street food scene has something for everyone. One of the most popular dishes in Penang is char kway teow, a stir-fried noodle dish made with flat rice noodles, prawns, bean sprouts, and Chinese sausage.

Relax on Batu Ferringhi beach

Batu Ferringhi is most popular for its sandy beaches and it draws endless streams of tourist every year. Along this winding stretch of beach, you will find a host of fun activities such as water sports and horse riding. The area comes to life with a night bazaar when the sun goes down.

Accommodations

Heritage Hotels

Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia's bustling capital, is renowned for its luxury accommodations that offer world-class amenities and unparalleled comfort. Whether you're looking for modern elegance or timeless sophistication, Kuala Lumpur's luxury hotels provide a lavish retreat in the heart of the city.

Beachfront Resorts

Penang's pristine coastline offers a range of stunning beachfront resorts that combine luxury with the beauty of the sea. These resorts provide a perfect escape for those looking to unwind by the beach, with direct access to sandy shores and breathtaking ocean views.

Langkawi

A tropical paradise with pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and duty-free shopping.

Ride the Langkawi Cable Car

The Langkawi Cable Car, also known as Langkawi SkyCab, is a gondola lift and one of the major attractions in Langkawi Island, Kedah, Malaysia. It provides an aerial link from the Oriental Village at Teluk Burau to the peak of Gunung Machinchang, which is also the location of the Langkawi Sky Bridge.

Relax on Cenang Beach

Langkawi's most tourist-friendly beach. This 2km stretch of soft white sand is lined with coconut trees, beach umbrellas, jet ski vendors, and beach bars. By day, sunbathers, swimmers, and watersport enthusiasts dominate the sparkling shoreline. As the sun softly sets, a series of beach bars become the center of attention as beanbags and cocktail menus are brought onto the sand.

Take a mangrove tour

The Tanjung Rhu Mangrove Tour in Langkawi is a must-do experience for nature lovers. The tour offers a captivating exploration of the mangrove forests, showcasing the area's unique wildlife and stunning landscapes. The knowledgeable guides provide insightful commentary about the ecosystem and conservation efforts.

Accommodations

Luxury Resorts

Langkawi is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and offers a selection of luxurious resorts that provide an unparalleled level of comfort and elegance. Ideal for those seeking a high-end experience, these resorts offer world-class amenities, exceptional service, and breathtaking views.

Budget Chalets

Langkawi, known for its beautiful beaches and lush landscapes, also offers a range of budget-friendly chalets that provide a comfortable and affordable stay. These chalets offer a cozy retreat without breaking the bank, making them an excellent choice for budget-conscious travelers.

Borneo

Home to diverse wildlife, ancient rainforests, and indigenous cultures.

Climb Mount Kinabalu

Standing majestically at 4,095m (13,435 feet above sea level), Mount Kinabalu is the highest mountain between the Himalayas and New Guinea. Mount Kinabalu derives its name from the Kadazan word, 'Aki Nabalu', meaning 'the revered place of the dead'.

Visit orangutans in Sepilok

Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre is located about 25 kilometres west of Sandakan in the state of Sabah, Malaysia. The centre opened in 1964 as the first official orangutan rehabilitation project for rescued orphaned baby orangutans from logging sites, plantations, illegal hunting or kept as pets.

Explore Mulu National Park

The park is dominated by Gunung Mulu, a 2,377 m-high sandstone pinnacle. At least 295 km of explored caves provide a spectacular sight and are home to millions of cave swiftlets and bats. The Sarawak Chamber, 600 m by 415 m and 80 m high, is the largest known cave chamber in the world.

Accommodations

Eco-lodges

Borneo's eco-lodges offer a unique way to experience the island's rich biodiversity and stunning natural landscapes while embracing sustainable practices. These lodges are ideal for nature enthusiasts and eco-conscious travelers looking to immerse themselves in Borneo's rainforests, wildlife, and cultural heritage.

Longhouse Homestays

Borneo's longhouse homestays offer a truly authentic experience, immersing travelers in the traditional lifestyle and culture of the indigenous communities. These homestays are perfect for those seeking to understand the rich cultural heritage of Borneo while enjoying warm hospitality and a unique living experience.
