About Malaysia

Discover the vibrant tapestry of cultures, rich history, and natural wonders of Malaysia

Overview

Malaysia is a diverse and multicultural country in Southeast Asia, known for its stunning landscapes, rich heritage, and modern cities. Divided into Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia (on Borneo), the country offers a unique blend of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous cultures.

Culture

Malaysia's culture is a vibrant tapestry of diverse ethnic traditions, languages, and religions, harmoniously blending Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences.

Festivals

Experience colorful celebrations like Hari Raya, Chinese New Year, Deepavali, and Gawai.

Arts and Crafts

Discover traditional batik, woodcarving, and pewter craftsmanship.

Languages

Hear the melodious mix of Malay, Chinese dialects, Tamil, and indigenous languages.

History

Malaysia's history is a journey from ancient trade, through colonial rule, to a diverse and independent nation.

Ancient Times

Early Malay kingdoms and maritime trade influence

15th Century

Establishment of the Malacca Sultanate

16th-20th Century

Colonial era under Portuguese, Dutch, and British rule

1957

Independence of Malaya

1963

Formation of Malaysia, including Sabah and Sarawak

Geography

Malaysia's geography features a stunning mix of tropical rainforests, mountains, and coastal areas, divided by the South China Sea into Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia on Borneo Island.

Rainforests

Home to some of the world's oldest tropical rainforests, including Taman Negara.

Mountains

Features Mount Kinabalu, the highest peak in Southeast Asia.

Islands

Boasts beautiful islands like Langkawi, Penang, and Tioman.

Economy

Malaysia has a rapidly developing economy, transitioning from a primarily agricultural and commodity-based economy to one focused on manufacturing and services.

  • Major industries: Electronics, automotive, palm oil production
  • Growing sectors: Islamic finance, biotechnology, renewable energy
  • Tourism: A significant contributor to the economy

People and Lifestyle

Malaysians are known for their hospitality, diverse culinary traditions, and harmonious multicultural society.

Cuisine

Explore a melting pot of flavors from various cultural influences.

Lifestyle

Experience the contrast between modern city life and traditional rural communities.

Sports

Enjoy popular sports like badminton, football, and sepak takraw.
